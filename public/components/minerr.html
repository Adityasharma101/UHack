Mineral Mapping In India

Humans have been altering the physical environment for thousands of years, whether it be through clearing land for agriculture, damming streams to divert water, or mining to extract minerals. The development of individual human beings, regions, and civilizations relies on various multifarious resources, including mining. Mining is a resource and equipment-intensive industry at the same time. It is stated that India is endowed with influential mineral resources. It spawns 89 minerals, of which 4 are Fuel Minerals, 11 Metallics, 52 Non-Metallic, and 22 Minor Minerals. Since these resources are such a large part of our daily lives, managing them becomes essential. Despite traditional means, the process of seeking minerals is still not instantaneous. There is a lot of difficulty in finding economically viable mineral reserves.

The iterative process of accumulating various datasets, followed by geological interpretation, takes a long period during exploration. Massive volumes of data are gathered and analyzed, frequently with no notable mineral discoveries. As a result, this analysis provides novel tactics and processes that will tremendously increase finding rates and shorten the exploration life cycle.
